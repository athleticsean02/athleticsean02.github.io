    • Interesting Facts

    Out of 35,402 people, within the year of 2016, 80.2% were males that had HIV
    19.8% were females

    Out of those 35,402 people, 70.2% were African American
    18.6% were White
    6.7% were Hispanic/Latino

    Out of 1,513 new patients diagnosed with HIV/AIDS in 2016, 83.3% were male
    16.7% were male

    Out of those 1,513 new patients:
    73.6% were African American
    7.9% were Hispanic/Latino
    14.5% were White

    The rate of Black males living with an HIV diagnosis is 4.8 times that of White males.
    The rate of Black females living with an HIV diagnosis is 14.7 times that of White females.

    The rate of Hispanic/Latino males living with an HIV diagnosis is 1.4 times that of White males.
    The rate of Hispanic/Latina females living with an HIV diagnosis is 4.2 times that of White females.
